TRIC-SAE: Triple-Control Speech Attribute Editing of Phoneme, Speaker, and Emotion

Paper(TBA) | Code(TBA) | Dataset(TBA)

Abstract

Speech carries rich linguistic and paralinguistic cues, and editing them enables applications in dubbing and voice personalization. While prior work typically modifies one attribute at a time, we introduce Speech Attribute Editing (SAE), a unified task for jointly editing multiple speech attributes. We propose TRIC-SAE, a diffusion-based framework that enables triple control over phoneme content, speaker identity and emotion. TRIC-SAE is trained on a large corpus spanning 87 speakers and 5 emotions. It leverages phonetic posteriorgrams (PPGs) for explicit content editing, and disentangles speaker and emotion representations using a contrastive cross-entropy (CCE) loss. Notably, the CCE loss achieves disentanglement without gradient reversal. Experimental results demonstrate that TRIC-SAE generates high-quality speech with precise and flexible control, outperforming baselines in both objective and subjective evaluations.
